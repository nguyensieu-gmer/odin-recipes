Coquito



Descriptions

This coquito recipe makes a creamy, tropical, Puerto Rican holiday drink with tons of coconut flavor. It's made with both evaporated and condensed milk, cream of coconut, spices, rum, and, in this version, egg yolks. It is always requested at my holiday gatherings. Sometimes I hide it in the fridge to keep for myself. Serve in glass cups and sprinkle with more cinnamon, if desired. Feliz Navidad!



Ingredients

  • 1 (12 fluid ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 2 large egg yolks, beaten
  • 1 (14 ounce) can cream of coconut
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup white rum
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves


Steps

  1. Step 1

    Combine evaporated milk and egg yolks in the top of a double boiler over simmering water. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture coats the back of a spoon and reaches a temperature of 160 degrees F (71
    degrees C).

  2. Step 2

    Transfer mixture to a blender. Add cream of coconut, sweetened condensed milk, rum, water, vanilla, cinnamon, and cloves; blend until well combined, about 30 seconds.

  3. Step 3

    Pour into a sterilized glass bottle and refrigerate. Serve over ice, if desired.
